<p><p>Grid-side energy storage refers to the use of storage technologies to manage the fluctuation of electricity supply and demand on the power grid. Peak-to-valley arbitrage involves storing excess energy during low demand periods and selling it during high demand periods, maximizing profit. Stored energy is used during times of high demand or emergencies, providing backup power. Peak shaving aims to reduce the peak demand on the grid by discharging stored energy during periods of high demand. Frequency modulation involves adjusting the frequency of stored energy to match the grid's requirements, ensuring stable and reliable power supply.</p></p>
